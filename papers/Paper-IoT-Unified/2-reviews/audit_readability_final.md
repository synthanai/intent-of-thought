## 🏆 Top 3 Strengths
1. **Unassailable Intellectual Honesty (10):** Declaring outright in the Conclusion and Introduction that "chain-of-thought remains an unreasonably robust default" and explicitly limiting the framework's claim to "governance uplift... not optimal topology prescription" is incredibly rare in AI literature. It builds immediate, unshakable trust.
2. **Empirical Evidence Weight (10):** The paper doesn't rely on vibes. Backing the claims with "705 scored reasoning episodes spanning 7 models... 18 high-stakes tasks across 6 domains" provides massive structural authority.
3. **Closing Resonance & Hook (9):** The Introduction hook identifies a "silent hazard" perfectly, and the Conclusion delivers a highly shareable, paradigm-level thesis: *"The locus of engineering value must shift from merely prompting for answers toward architecting reliable intent structures."*

## 📈 Top 3 Opportunities
1. **Economy (8):** As an academic paper, there is inherent density. While highly readable for cs.CL, extracting a pure layman summary (a "TL;DR" or visual abstract tweet thread) will be necessary for wider distribution on LinkedIn/X.
2. **Emotional Arc (7):** Inherently constrained by the academic format, though the narrative tension between "catastrophic drift" (the problem) and the "safety net" (the solution) carries the intellectual intrigue well.
3. **Actionability (9):** Strong for engineers (who can immediately start using the Purpose / Anti-Purpose / Success Signal triple). You could explicitly call out a 1-paragraph "How to implement this today" in future blog teardowns of the paper.
